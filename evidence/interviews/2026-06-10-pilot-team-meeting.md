# Pilot team meeting — how the researcher actually wants to work with AI

## What was observed / claimed

The maintainer met with the pilot researcher (the other member of the pilot team) to discuss how the methodology's first month of contact had gone and how the researcher wants to work. This is the conversation channel of the pilot feeding back (see `../../case-studies/snn-research/README.md`); it follows the same-day background audit (`../observations/2026-06-10-pilot-workspace-audit.md`) and is consistent with what that audit found in the repo state.

## Specific claims

- **Chat is the comfortable interface.** The researcher's preferred way of interacting with AI is conversation. He does not want to author documents himself; asked to fill or maintain files, he won't. (Consistent with the audit: INTAKE deleted, ledgers untouched.)
- **The working relationship he wants: AI as a junior colleague.** Trust its input incompletely — but it is fine, even desired, for it to do all the heavy lifting of the technical work. (Consistent with the audit's "AI-as-full-team" working-mode note, stated here in the researcher's own framing.)
- **He stayed engaged through conversation.** The teach→quiz interactions were carried entirely in chat and were the part of the methodology that visibly worked for him (the audit independently shows the quiz gate was kept after the posture was relaxed, and caught a real gap).

## Implications (suggested, not concluded)

- Bears on **A16/A17** and on the framing of the methodology's central response: a researcher who will not write documents but will converse extensively *might* still build the second output if the building happens in conversation — the practice question becomes how to carry verification, state, and reflection through chat-shaped interactions rather than researcher-authored artifacts. This is the question the posture revision (logged separately) stakes itself on; opened as Q13.
- Bears on **A5/A6/A7**: "junior colleague, never fully trusted" is the researcher's own restatement of cluster II — suggesting the verification disciplines are the part of the methodology that survives contact with how researchers want to work, while authorship rules are the part that does not.
- The document-authoring asks (intake, ledgers) *might* be the methodology's main adoption tax; interface forms that replace them with conversation (interview-style onboarding, gate conversations, agent-maintained records) are the suggested direction.

## Notes / caveats

- n=1 researcher, in one field, who is also the maintainer's teammate; his preferences may not generalize. The lab survey and future interviews carry the generalization question.
- Notes relayed by the maintainer from memory, same day, no transcript. The maintainer is a pilot-team member; selection and framing bias apply.
- The design decisions made in response to this meeting are deliberately *not* recorded here — they are decisions, not observations, and live in the log entry of the same date (`../../log/2026-06-10-posture-revision-and-redesign.md`).
