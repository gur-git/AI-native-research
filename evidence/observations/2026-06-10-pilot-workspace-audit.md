# First background audit of the pilot workspace

## What was observed / claimed

The pilot research workspace (the team's SNN/neuromorphic effort, scaffolded 2026-06-03 from `HANDOFF.md` v0 at pin `fc00c47`) was read end to end: front matter (`README.md`, `CLAUDE.md`, `GOAL.md`, `state.md`, `FRICTION.md`), all seven repo skills, and the working content (`knowledge/`, `context/`, `ideation/`, `ledgers/`). This is the first run of the background channel by which the pilot feeds the inquiry: periodic maintainer-side reads of the pilot repo, recorded here as observations. It is a snapshot of a single shallow commit; activity content is dated through 2026-06-05.

Two events dominate the snapshot. First, within two days of adopting the methodology (decision D001, 2026-06-03, itself still flagged "proposed — team to confirm"), the team chose an **"AI-as-full-team" working mode** — AI "does the dirty work, teaches, and helps them ideate, understand, and process thoughts" — which the team's own state file says "contradicts CLAUDE.md § methodology, the re-aimed skills, and GOAL § Two outputs"; reconciliation is explicitly deferred ("tune later") and the repo is "intentionally in a half-state." `HANDOFF.md` and `INTAKE.md` were deleted (their researcher-authored content transcribed into `state.md` first), and the autonomous `/progress` driver and a `WORK_LOG.md` stub were retired. An agent-drafted friction entry calls the posture reversal "(The biggest signal for the source methodology.)"

Second, the team built and exercised a **teach→quiz loop** that no current guideline owns (built in parallel with — and plausibly influenced by — their supervising professor's mentoring practice; see the caveat below). The `learn` skill has the agent map terrain (sources, structure, the questions a note must answer) while refusing to author the load-bearing mechanism/derivation/intuition "even if asked to just draft it"; the `quiz` skill then generates 4–7 understanding-not-recall questions from the concept note (≥1 derivation, ≥1 "why / what breaks if…", none answerable by pattern-matching the note's wording), withholds answers, grades the team's answers correct/partial/wrong "with the right answer and where the note covers it" ("Be honest; a generous pass defeats the purpose"), and only a genuine pass closes a node in the knowledge map ("no self-grading by Claude, no pass-by-default"). The loop caught a real gap on its second use — a steady-state derivation failed on first attempt and passed on re-attempt after a targeted hint — and the team kept the quiz gate even after relaxing the authoring posture. Notably, the skill cites the methodology's G3 for legitimacy, but inverts G3's direction: G3 verifies *AI output* against an independent source; the quiz verifies *the researcher's understanding*, with the agent as prober and grader. The failure branch is bidirectional: "if the miss was caused by an unclear explanation, improve the concept note too."

## Specific claims

- **Posture reversal.** The methodology's central posture (advise-don't-author) was adopted and then relaxed within ~2 days in favor of AI-as-full-team; the empower/replace map was kept but demoted to "a soft guide." Reconciliation is deferred, not resolved. Per an agent-drafted (unadopted) friction entry, the intake had surfaced the contradiction (Protect = ideation/understanding vs. "AI as my entire team") and the verdict went against the scaffold's posture; the working-mode choice itself is corroborated by the team-owned `state.md`.
- **Verification held while authorship rules fell.** The teach→quiz gate and citation hygiene are the disciplines the snapshot shows in active, effective use: a real understanding gap caught (F2), real mis-citations caught and corrected (e.g., CLP-SNN misattributed), honest-baseline and calibrated-uncertainty rules visibly practiced in the notes.
- **Commitment is mentor-gated.** The pilot's phase structure reserves the exit from its ideation phase — committing a research question — to a human-reserved decision made together with the supervising mentor (pilot `GOAL.md`, §The phase structure and its gate table).
- **Adoption is uneven by discipline.** Knowledge-layer disciplines (external state in `state.md` — used heavily and well; source citation with accessed dates and verification marks) are strong. The reflective ledgers (G7 calibration, G11 judgment) contain only header rows — zero entries despite being the cheapest-by-design practices — and their retention is an open question in the pilot. G10 source-walking has not yet begun as of the snapshot: all 7 agent-produced paper scaffolds await the team's green/yellow/red walk and judgment sections (two days elapsed, during which the team worked the map's recommended first concept batch — "not yet begun" is the observable fact; "stalled" would overread it).
- **The interface was re-cut on contact.** What the pilot itself re-cut: it did not adopt the methodology's 0–7 advisory path, keeping its own 3-phase *locked-gate* structure rooted in its pre-existing plans ("the gates are not advisory" — stricter than the methodology's offered path, not looser); the prescribed thin one-screen CLAUDE.md was rejected for a rich domain+methodology file; HANDOFF-as-persistent-file proved redundant once CLAUDE.md carried the pin; INTAKE functioned as a one-shot diagnostic rather than a persistent artifact. (The nine-discipline checklist in the pilot's CLAUDE.md is *not* a pilot re-cut: HANDOFF v0 itself prescribes exactly those nine disciplines as a compact checklist. The pilot kept that form near-verbatim — a data point that the compressed form was usable, attributable to the handoff's design, not to the pilot.)
- **Emergent practices the methodology does not prescribe:** a dependency-graph knowledge map with three node states (☐/◐/✓) where ✓ requires a passed self-test; agent-side refusal lines that enforce the disciplines against the researcher's own in-the-moment requests; a "drafted observation / human verdict" convention (the agent drafts candidate friction and state entries inside clearly-marked proposal blocks, verdicts left blank); a verify-before-cite STOP rule (an unresolvable arXiv ID/DOI halts the deep-read); inline authorship/status headers (agent-drafted vs. team-owned) — a lighter, partial substitute for the prescribed G15 provenance forms (`.aimeta.md` / commit message), which are not visibly practiced.
- **The friction channel exists but has not been exercised by the researcher.** All friction entries are agent-drafted candidates in a comment block awaiting the team's helped/chafed/ignored verdicts; nothing has flowed back to this repo through the prescribed issue/PR route. (This background audit is itself the workaround.)

## Implications (suggested, not concluded)

- The quiz loop bears on **A16** and **A13** and converges with the supervising professor's classical explain-back practice (`../interviews/2026-06-10-professor-classical-research-steps.md`): two *convergent* sources pointing at the same practice — researcher explains, a prober checks — as both a knowledge gate and a second-output measure. The convergence is not full independence: the professor supervises this same pilot team (the quiz gate may descend from his mentoring culture), and both sources reach this repo through the maintainer, who is a pilot-team member. This *might* support an explain-back guideline that owns the practice rather than letting it ride on a stretched G3 citation. It is also a live candidate answer to **Q11**.
- The posture reversal bears on **A16/A17** and on the framing of G1: it is the most direct contact-with-reality signal recorded so far, but it is one team, two days in, with the team's own reconciliation deferred — evidence to weigh with the team in conversation, not a verdict on the principle. It also bears on **Q9/Q10** (whether the methodology's defensive posture fits how researchers actually want to work).
- The empty ledgers bear on **G6/G7/G11** design and **Q11**: cost was designed to minutes, yet adoption was zero — the barrier appears to be trigger/ownership, not cost.
- The not-yet-begun paper-scaffold handoff bears on **G10/G13**: pre-filling a starting graph *may* crowd out the team's source-walk rather than seed it — too early to say at two days.
- The re-cut interface (the pilot's own gated 3-phase structure kept over the offered path, rich CLAUDE.md, transient INTAKE, redundant HANDOFF file) is direct evidence for the "interface under study" question and *might* motivate a HANDOFF v1.
- The pilot surfaced a genuine tension between "pause at substantive moments" (discipline 9 / G1) and rules for autonomous agent runs; the methodology currently does not say how its checkpoints apply when the agent runs unattended.

## Notes / caveats
