# Contributing

This repo is a public, evolving inquiry into AI-native research methodology, maintained by one researcher. It is **not currently accepting external contributions** — pull requests will be politely declined.

## Why read-only

The repo is in its early phase. The foundation is being written, the methodology is being grown from evidence, and the maintainer needs to make many small choices about voice, structure, and direction without coordinating them. External contributions at this stage would compound rather than help.

This may change later. The repo will signal it explicitly if and when it does.

## What you can do

- **Read along.** The foundation documents (`foundation/`), the methodology ([`METHODOLOGY.md`](METHODOLOGY.md)), and the log (`log/`) are written to be read.
- **Use the methodology.** The starter workspace (linked from the README once published) is the way to run your own research with it; your workspace's `FRICTION/` folder is the feedback channel — early users' friction is read by arrangement.
- **Watch / star** if you want to follow the inquiry.
- **Reach out** if you are working on related problems and would like to discuss a collaboration. Email: **masuryg@post.bgu.ac.il**.

Useful kinds of outreach:

- You are a working researcher who has been using AI seriously for at least a few months and would be willing to be interviewed about your actual workflow.
- You are running a structured experiment (n=1 or larger) on AI's effect on your research and would consider sharing results.
- You are working on a pilot project where this methodology could be applied, and want to talk about whether the timing works.
- You disagree with a foundation claim and have evidence that should be weighed against it.

Useful kinds of outreach are about substance, not about the repo itself. "I'd like to contribute to your methodology" without a specific shape is hard to act on; "I ran this experiment and the result contradicts your A6" is easy to act on.

## Citing

If the repo's framing or a specific document is useful in your own work, citation is welcome. See `CITATION.cff` for a starting point.
