# 00-why-reimagine.md rewritten as a walk-through (2026-05-06)

## What prompted it

The previous version of `00-why-reimagine.md` opened by addressing the reader directly — "if you are reading this, you have likely noticed..." — and asked the reader to recognize themselves in a set of pre-supplied observations before laying out the argument. The maintainer found this lecturing and presumptuous. The right structure is a walk-through: describe how research has worked, describe how technology has historically changed knowledge work, then show how AI breaks the pattern, then show why this matters for research, then propose the response.

## What changed

The document now has seven sections in the order the maintainer requested:

1. **What research has been.** Research's structure as a kind of knowledge work; the core/periphery split; the property that the researcher's growing competence is itself a primary output (two outputs, not one); the historical cost structure (production expensive, judgment cheap).
2. **How technology has historically changed knowledge work.** The reliable pattern: tools displace skills and produce reciprocal skills that compound. Calculator → calculator-literacy. Compiler → higher-level fluency. GPS → route-planning judgment. Names the underlying property that made the pattern work: prior tools required *informationally substantial* input from the user, and so using the tool well was itself the new skill.
3. **How AI is different.** The asymmetry between thin natural-language input and fluent output. AI does not require the kind of articulate input that produces reciprocal skill. The structural claim: AI is the first tool that can substitute for skill without requiring reciprocal skill development. Three things follow: outsourcing is a trap, tasks resist clean decomposition, the deliverable and the worker are different optimization targets.
4. **Why this forces reimagining research specifically.** Four reasons drawn from §1: research's defining operation is producing claims that did not exist before; production decomposes into recombination (where AI fits) and innovation (where it does not); research has the researcher's growing competence as a primary output; research has higher stakes for fluent-but-empty output. Carries the BVSR / smart-diversification / parallel-exploitation-engine framing from the previous version.
5. **Why now.** Adoption is happening with or without methodology. The gradient pulls toward replacement. Defaults are forming. Inertia is not neutral.
6. **The mindset shift: empower, not replace.** Same content as the previous version's §5. The central call to action.
7. **What this repo is.** Structure of the inquiry, references to other documents, expectation of permanent revision.

## What is preserved

All the strong notions from prior conversations remain present:

- AI substitutes for skill without reciprocal skill (§3, central claim)
- The outsourcing trap (§3)
- Tasks resist clean decomposition (§3)
- Two outputs: deliverable and researcher (§1, §3, §4)
- Innovation as a categorically different operation, BVSR structure, smart diversification (§4)
- AI as parallel exploitation engine, not parallel exploration engine (§4)
- The cost-structure claim, with the qualification that it applies cleanly to recombination (§4)
- Research is innovation-heavy in a way most knowledge work is not (§4)
- Urgency from the gradient and forming defaults (§5)
- Empower not replace as central mindset (§6)
- Methodology never finished (§7)

## What changed in approach

The previous version started with assertions about what the reader had noticed. The new version starts with a description of the world (research, then the historical pattern of tools and skills) and lets the reader follow the argument to the conclusion. Each section is meant to be a step that can be checked before the next.

The historical-tools section (§2) is new in detail. The previous version mentioned calculator/compiler/GPS as a list; the new version walks through each, names the underlying property that makes the pattern work (informationally substantial input → reciprocal skill), and uses that property to set up §3's claim that AI breaks the pattern by accepting informationally thin input.

§4's treatment of innovation also reads more cleanly because §1 now sets up the two-outputs claim explicitly — when §4 returns to it, the reader has the prior context.
