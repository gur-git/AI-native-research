# A speculative v0 of the AI-native research process (2026-06-01)

Same day as the self-evidence stream opened, the maintainer chose to pause evidence-gathering and
first draft what the AI-native research process might *look like*, built from current understanding —
the foundation, the 15 (still `proposed`) guidelines, and a read of AI's trajectory — on the
assumption that the goal of research is unchanged. The rationale: a connective picture of the whole,
even a speculative one, helps decide what evidence to go get. This is consistent with the repo's
"first imperfect version" stance, *provided* the draft is honest about its status.

## What was done

Produced [`../methodology/ai-native-research-process.md`](../methodology/ai-native-research-process.md)
— a `v0` draft of the end-to-end process, explicitly marked speculative, subordinate to the
foundation, with every non-trivial claim tagged `grounded` / `inferred` / `speculative`.

Method (multi-agent workflow, 18 agents): ground in the repo's own materials → draft the process
from five independent organizing lenses (research-lifecycle, bottleneck-inversion, two-outputs,
AI-trajectory, empower/replace-operational) → adversarially critique and score each → synthesize the
best into one document → check for completeness, empower/replace integrity, and tone → repair.

The integrity/completeness critics found no fabrications, no banned (promotional) words, and no
whole-job replacement violations; their residual findings (attribute-tag fidelity, missing lifecycle
phases, an over-reached "same axis" theoretical claim, citation-address precision, an internal
self-inconsistency) were applied in the repair pass. Two post-hoc cleanups were done by hand: a UTF-8
re-extraction, and removal of two internal "House Rule" references that leaked from the workflow
prompt.

## Shape of the draft

The process is organized as: the unchanged goal (two outputs — artifact and researcher) → the core
reframe (the five diagnosis clusters, each redirecting the work) → ten phases (0a choose what to
study, 0b load state & frame, 1 classify task, 2 diverge then select-against-own-ranking-first, 3
design with verification promoted to the front, 4 execution on non-decomposable tasks, 5 verify from
an independent source, 6 carry state & instrument both outputs, 7 prune portfolio & protect
exploration, 8 disseminate, 9 respond to review) → cross-cutting practices mapped to G1–G15 → a
"serves both outputs" double-booking → honest limitations, each paired with the evidence stream that
would test it.

## What it surfaced for the inquiry (maintainer decisions, not taken here)

- **A candidate new guideline** the process leans on but no existing guideline covers: a *scheduled
  re-examination of the foundation's attribute tags against the current model generation* — deciding
  on a cadence which practices address now-closing limitations (and can be retired as scaffolding)
  versus which trace to structural attributes that capability gains *widen*. A14 names the need
  ("designed revisability"); no G-number operationalizes it. Candidate for `add-guideline` once an
  attribute + evidence support it.
- **Lifecycle phases the draft only sketches:** empirical data generation (Phase 4) and external peer
  review (Phase 9). The arc is shaped like a theory/math/code workflow, inheriting the foundation's
  own domain bias.
- **The project → portfolio shift (Phase 7)** is flagged `speculative` — real, or does research stay
  project-shaped?
- **The centaur/cyborg vocabulary decision** (adopt/refine/contest) remains deferred and now blocks
  cleaner positioning.

No guideline statuses were changed. The draft is a hypothesis the evidence streams exist to test.
