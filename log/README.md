# log/

The chronicle. What happened in this inquiry, when, and why.

## What lives here

One file per day on which something substantive happened. Filename: `YYYY-MM-DD-{topic}.md` where `{topic}` is a short kebab-case slug capturing the day's theme. Multiple files per day are fine if the topics are distinct.

A log entry typically contains one or more of:

- A piece of evidence added (with link).
- A foundation update (with before/after, the evidence that drove it, and a one-paragraph reason).
- A guideline added or moved between statuses (with link, status, motivation).
- A direction shift, decision, or change of mind, with reasoning.
- A note on something noticed in real working sessions that hasn't yet been formalized into evidence.

The skills (`add-evidence`, `update-foundation`, `add-guideline`) write to the log automatically. Direct edits to the log are also welcome — many shifts in an inquiry are discursive and don't slot into a skill.

## Log vs guidelines

Two things that often get conflated, kept separate here:

- **Log entries** are descriptive — what happened on a date. Append-only journal.
- **Guidelines** in `methodology/guidelines.md` are normative — what to do. Cumulative position.

The log is where a *story* of the inquiry is recorded. Guidelines are extracted from the story when patterns become clear enough to prescribe. The transition is deliberate: a guideline does not get drafted directly; it gets drafted from log entries that have earned it.

## Tone

Short. Specific. Dated. Linkable. The log is the place where future-you (or a future collaborator) reconstructs *why* a choice was made, so it should over-explain reasoning rather than under-explain it. But it should not be precious — a log entry is not a publication.

## What the log is not

- Not a daily diary. Quiet days don't need entries.
- Not a marketing channel. Don't write log entries for an audience.
- Not a place for half-formed methodology. If a thought is becoming a guideline, log the *observation* that motivated it; the guideline gets drafted via `add-guideline` separately.
