# case-studies/

Real research projects that this methodology gets applied to. Empty for now — the maintainer's pilot research domain has not been chosen yet.

## What will live here

When the pilot starts, this directory will contain:

- The research question and its evolution over time.
- The artifacts produced (code, data, papers) where they can be public.
- A continuous log of what worked and what didn't, written in a form a future reader can follow.
- The AI-contribution ledger for each artifact (which models, which prompts, what was accepted and rejected).

The case studies are where the methodology either earns its keep or doesn't. A methodology that cannot survive contact with a real project is opinion. A methodology that produces work the maintainer would defer to from a colleague has earned a position.

## Why this is a directory and not a TODO

Naming the directory now is a commitment. The repo's identity rests on the methodology being applied, not just described. Every visitor who reads the README and clicks through can see this is meant to be filled — and can come back to see whether it has been.

## When the pilot starts

A new subdirectory `case-studies/{slug}/` gets created with its own README explaining the project, its question, its constraints, and how it relates to the methodology. The first log entry under `log/` for the day the pilot starts records the choice and reasoning.
