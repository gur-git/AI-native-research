# foundation/

The diagnosis. This is where the inquiry's claims about *why* research must be reimagined live, and what about AI forces that reimagining.

## Files

- **`00-why-reimagine.md`** — the argument that AI is restructuring knowledge work generally, that production is at least two qualitatively different operations (recombination and innovation) which AI affects differently, that research is a particular case worth treating on its own, and why the old model of research becomes less effective in this regime.
- **`01-ai-attributes.md`** — the catalog of AI attributes that any AI-native research methodology must reckon with. Each attribute is tagged `structural`, `temporary`, or `ambiguous`, with a justification.
- **`02-what-this-implies.md`** — what the diagnosis gestures at, in terms of how a reimagined workflow might look. Explicitly a gesture, not a methodology. The methodology lives in `methodology/` and is built up from evidence.
- **`03-open-questions.md`** — things the foundation does not yet have a settled view on, with notes on what would resolve them.
- **`04-innovation.md`** — the deeper treatment of innovation as a categorically distinct operation from recombination, drawing on Campbell's BVSR theory, March's exploration/exploitation distinction, Stanley's novelty search, and Sarasvathy's effectuation. Argues that AI's cost reduction applies cleanly to recombination but not to innovation, with implications for how the methodology must distinguish the two.

## How this directory changes

Foundation files are normative — they make claims about how things are. They change when evidence in `evidence/` accumulates against a current claim or extends it. Use the `update-foundation` skill rather than editing these files ad-hoc; the skill enforces the traceability trail that keeps the foundation auditable.

Each file ends with a *Last revised* footnote that points to the evidence that drove the most recent change. The log in `log/` carries the longer history.
