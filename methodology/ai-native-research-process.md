# A draft of the AI-native research process (speculative v0)

> **Phase-scheme note.** The phase scheme an agent actually operates on is the reconciled 0–7 arc in [`agent-operating-guide.md`](agent-operating-guide.md) (Orient · Context · Survey-then-focus · Design · Execute · Verify · Disseminate · Respond). This document is the longer descriptive treatment, still written around an older `0a–9` decomposition and being reconciled to the 0–7 scheme; where they differ, the operating guide is current.

> **STATUS — SPECULATIVE v0 SYNTHESIS.** This document is a reasoned construction from the repo's current foundation, not an evidence-backed result. It is subordinate to [`GOAL.md`](../GOAL.md) and to `foundation/`; where it appears to conflict with them, they are authoritative. Every guideline it composes (G1–G15) is at status `proposed` — none is yet supported by systematic evidence. The whole document is a hypothesis the repo exists to test, not a finding it has. Every non-trivial claim is tagged inline: **(grounded)** = traces to a named attribute (A1–A17), a guideline (G1–G15), or a cited source; **(inferred)** = a reasoned extension of grounded material; **(speculative)** = a bet about AI capability or its trajectory. The phase decomposition itself is an inferential scaffold: the individual moves are mostly grounded, but their arrangement into an ordered arc is **(inferred)**, and several attribute tags it leans on are partially-closing — flagged in place rather than presented as fixed.

## The unchanged goal

Research has two outputs, not one (grounded; GOAL.md §What changed about research). The first is the **artifact** — the paper, dataset, proof, code. The second is the **researcher**: "a person who can do harder work next time than they could last time" (grounded; GOAL.md §What changed about research). This process does not change either; it changes only *how* the work is done given that AI exists. The end remains reliable, novel, valuable knowledge plus a developing researcher (by assumption; the research goal is held fixed).

A third claim from the same passage links the two outputs and is easy to lose: skill is "what separates AI slop from work a researcher should be willing to put their name on," so "the benefit is therefore double — better output now, and growing capacity that compounds" (grounded; GOAL.md §What changed about research). Skilled AI use is asserted to improve the *artifact now*, not only the researcher later. The process therefore owes an account of artifact quality as a tracked output, not just researcher development; the double-entry section below is built to carry both.

The second output is load-bearing for the design because of where it comes from: it "is built almost entirely through the routine peripheral work that surrounds any research project — reading, analyzing, drafting, testing, replicating" (grounded; GOAL.md §What changed about research). That periphery is the apprenticeship, and it is exactly the work AI is most fluent at absorbing. The structural risk the whole repo turns on: AI is "the first tool that does not insist" on reciprocal skill (grounded; A16, GOAL.md §What changed about research), so a researcher "can ship strong papers and quietly hollow out" and "will not notice" (grounded; GOAL.md §What changed about research). A process accountable only to the artifact would optimize the second output away silently. This one is accountable to both.

## The core reframe

AI does not reorder the research lifecycle; it changes the cost structure inside it, and the changes cluster into five diagnostic groups that each redirect the work.

**I — Asymmetric competence inverts the bottleneck.** Breadth is cheap (grounded; A1), cross-domain transfer near-free (grounded; A2), parallel attempts nearly costless (grounded; A3), and plausible ideas have dropped to roughly zero marginal cost (grounded; A4). For most of the history of inquiry, *generation* was expensive and *selection* comparatively cheap, because little got generated. That has flipped: "the new bottleneck is selection" (grounded; A4). The scarce human work is now choosing among cheap candidates, supplying the variation AI cannot reach, and verifying. One correction the inversion must carry: it is *not* "AI generates, human picks." AI is "a parallel exploitation engine more than a parallel exploration engine" — its variants cluster around the trained distribution, so *innovative* generation (out-of-distribution moves) did not get cheap (grounded; innovation-as-different-operation §3). The human still has to generate the out-of-distribution moves *and* select (inferred from A1, A4, innovation note). A2 is the one cluster-I attribute tagged a pure structural *capability* with no substitution risk — cheap cross-domain transfer — and the process should use it offensively (importing a technique from a neighboring field), not only defensively (mapping terrain). This is taken up at a checkpoint in Phase 2.

**II — Failure is subtle, fluent, and biased toward agreement.** AI's confidence does not track correctness (grounded; A5), it validates its own output by default (grounded; A6), it anchors to the user's framing (grounded; A7, *ambiguous, leaning structural*), it hallucinates most exactly at the recent-and-niche frontier where research happens (grounded; A8, *ambiguous, leaning structural*), and it produces candidates faster than anyone can verify them (grounded; A9). The response is to promote verification from a last step to a structural commitment that shapes earlier ones: decide what would convincingly verify *before* designing (grounded; cluster-II direction in `02-implications.md`).

**III — AI does not accumulate.** No memory across sessions by default (grounded; A10, *ambiguous*), no learning from a prior attempt (grounded; A11, *ambiguous*), inconsistent across runs (grounded; A12, *temporary*). The human becomes the carrier of cumulative state, held in version-controlled, re-loadable files (grounded; G2). Note the tags: A10 and A11 are *ambiguous* — "the AI side is closing" via persistent memory and long context (grounded; A10 tag) — not *temporary*; only A12 carries the *temporary* tag (grounded; A12 tag, line 125). The state-carrying practice survives the close anyway, for reasons independent of whether AI memory works: it is robust to model upgrades, vendor changes, and the researcher's own forgetting (grounded; A10 note). The reason is durable even though the literal limitation is not.

**IV — We cannot trust our own perception of AI.** Felt productivity is unreliable: METR's RCT found developers 19% slower while pre-task expecting a 24% speedup and post-task reporting a 20% speedup, a 39-point felt-vs-measured gap (grounded; A13). Capability moves fast and unevenly (grounded; A14); most adoption rests on impression, not measurement (grounded; A15, *cultural, not technical*). The response is crude personal measurement and designed revisability — time-stamped claims and retirable guidelines (grounded; A13, A14, A15; G7).

**V — AI changes the user.** Output arrives without reciprocal skill development (grounded; A16), and tasks resist clean decomposition — routine and original moves interleave inside the same sentence (grounded; A17). The response is to make the empower/replace question visible at the moments it is at stake, and to track the second output as deliberately as the first (grounded; G1, G6).

The synthesis of all five: **selection and innovation become the scarce human work; verification moves to the front; external version-controlled cognition carries state; calibration replaces felt productivity; skill preservation becomes a design criterion, not an afterthought.**

The controlling principle through all of it is **empower-not-replace, applied at the whole-job frame** (grounded; G1). The operational question is: *will my capacity to do the research job, at the framing the job is judged on, be greater because of how I use AI here, or smaller?* (grounded; G1, GOAL.md §The commitment). Most keystrokes never trigger it, and that is correct, not a loophole — routine syntax, citation formats, unit conversions sit below where research value is created and are fine to delegate (grounded; G1, GOAL.md §The commitment). The line is crossed only when AI's output would *be* the substantive contribution at the level the work is judged on. The line is not fixed: "the principle is stable; the line it draws moves with the technology" (grounded; GOAL.md §The commitment).

**Field positioning.** Five public positions exist (grounded; step-0-landscape.md): agentic delegation, augmentation-by-architecture, craft/skill-preservation, governance/cautionary, and silence. This process sits in **craft (Vector 3)** but as a *positive* construction. The craft camp's practicing scientists (Tao, Howard, Mineault, Pachter, Mollick) frame skill formation as a *warning* or an *open problem*, never as a positive design criterion (grounded; step-0-landscape.md line 209). The survey's headline finding: "no player is claiming … that AI use should be evaluated on whether it grows the researcher, rather than on whether it produces outputs"; "the repo's 'empower, not replace' stance in its explicit positive form has no direct claimant among 47 entities canvassed" (grounded; step-0-landscape.md line 209 — but provisional: the survey flags every position as pre-citation-grade and notes "'no explicit position found' is a step-0 finding, not a durable absence," step-0-landscape.md §5 lines 222–223, so this gap-claim is **pending verification**, not settled). The gap this process fills sits between Karpathy's *negative* "you can't outsource understanding" and a *positive* design criterion (grounded; step-0-landscape.md line 239, same provisional caveat). The centaur/cyborg vocabulary (Kasparov → Mollick) is the field's nearest existing handle for human/AI division of labor (grounded; step-0-landscape.md line 136); whether this repo adopts, refines, or contests it is a decision the maintainer has explicitly deferred (grounded; step-0-landscape.md line 234), so this draft uses the vocabulary only to locate itself, not as a committed term.

## The process, end to end

The phases below describe one recurring research cycle. They are presented in an order, but the order is a reasoned overlay on guidelines that are not themselves sequenced **(inferred at the structural level)**; research iterates and the phases recur and interleave. The arc as drawn is also shaped like a theory/math/code workflow — the foundation's own coverage is lighter on physics, chemistry, biology, and fieldwork (grounded; step-0-landscape.md line 226), and the process inherits that bias. Where empirical data generation and external review belong, the phases below flag that they are sketched, not worked.

Three facts discipline the whole arc:

- **The empower-replace line is not one line; it is one per phase, and it slopes** (inferred from G1, A17). The *direction* of the slope is reasoned — toward human ownership at selection, design, and verification, toward AI at generation, execution-filling, and reformatting. The *specific per-phase position* on that line is a bet **(speculative)**: A17 says the routine/original split is not cleanly locatable, which means a clean per-phase ordering of cut-points overreaches what the foundation supports. A flat "use AI carefully" rule is still wrong — too strict at execution, not strict enough at verification (inferred) — but the exact gradient below should be read as a hypothesis about how research labor divides, not a settled map.
- **The checkpoints are placed at the transitions between phases (speculative).** No attribute or guideline locates checkpoints; this is an architectural choice the document originates, and A17 arguably predicts the opposite — that the decisive checkpoint fires mid-sentence, inside a phase, not at its boundary (grounded; A17). The transition placement is a design bet that catches the *between-phase* substantive moments (what to generate, keep, verify, log) cheaply; Phase 4 is where it is most likely to fail, and the retrospective capability log (Phase 6) is the backstop for the mid-phase slips it misses.
- **The lifecycle has phases this draft does not yet work.** Problem/topic *selection* (which question to pursue) is treated below as Phase 0a but thinly; empirical *data generation* is folded into Phase 4 and flagged; *peer review and revision* is sketched as Phase 9; *collaboration and mentorship* is handled as a cross-cutting note. None is as worked as the frame→verify→state spine, and the gaps are named where they fall.

### Phase 0a — Choose what to study

- **Human role.** Decide which question or domain to pursue. This is the highest-stakes taste decision in the arc, and the one the foundation's open questions most directly point at — "direction-and-outcome" judgment (Q7, G11) and "what research becomes newly feasible because AI exists" (Q9). Generative opportunity-spotting belongs here: some research is worth doing *now, because AI exists*, that was not feasible before (e.g., importing a technique across a domain boundary that was previously too expensive to cross). A2 (cheap cross-domain transfer) is the structural capability that makes this move available (grounded; A2).
- **AI role.** Survey adjacent fields for transplantable techniques and surface where two literatures have not yet been connected — terrain, not selection (grounded; A2, G13).
- **The empower-replace line.** The *choice of question* is the substantive contribution and stays human; AI may widen the option set but does not pick. **Signal you are at a checkpoint:** you are about to pursue a direction because AI proposed it read as promising, not because you can say why it matters. Selecting the question is named in the charter among the skills the job is judged on (grounded; GOAL.md §The commitment).
- **Skill built or preserved.** Problem selection and opportunity-spotting — the scarcest taste in research.
- **Traces to.** A2, A4; G11, G13, G1; Q9.
- **(inferred / partly speculative)** — that some research is *newly feasible because of AI* is a bet about capability **(speculative)**; that the choice of question should be the front-of-lifecycle phase is a reasoned overlay (inferred). This phase is thinner than the spine and is flagged as such.

### Phase 0b — Load state and frame the question

- **Human role.** Re-ground a fresh session by reading the version-controlled project-state file, then write, in your own words, the (chosen) question, its stakes, and what would count as a convincing answer (grounded; G2).
- **AI role.** Consume the state file to re-orient; map the surrounding literature and adjacent framings as terrain so you can see where the map ends (grounded; G13, A2).
- **The empower-replace line.** The substantive contributions are the *question's framing* and the *judgment of what the state file should hold*. **Signal:** you are about to accept AI's phrasing of the research question, or let AI decide what is worth logging. Framing is named in the charter as a skill the job is judged on (grounded; GOAL.md §The commitment); accepting an AI-proposed framing is the substitution that hollows out taste, because idea generation is near-free and distribution-bound (grounded; A4, A16).
- **Skill built or preserved.** Question-framing; the craft of deciding what to log and at what grain (grounded; G2 skill implication).
- **Traces to.** A2, A4, A16, A10, A11; G2, G13, G1.
- **(inferred)** — the moves are grounded; their placement at the head of a cycle is a reasoned overlay.

### Phase 1 — Classify the task

- **Human role.** Before any non-trivial work, classify it: recombination (assembling known elements) or innovation (probing outside the trained distribution)? (grounded; G4).
- **AI role.** None yet — deliberately upstream of AI use.
- **The empower-replace line.** This is the meta-checkpoint that calibrates every later one. For recombination, AI-as-parallel-engine is usually fine and the dominant worry is verification; for innovation, AI is a partial-fit substrate and the line tightens toward "AI maps, human moves" (grounded; G4, innovation note §3). **Signal:** you cannot say out loud which kind of operation the next hour is. When in doubt, treat as innovation: over-classifying costs friction, under-classifying lets AI substitute for the developing part (grounded; G4).
- **Skill built or preserved.** In-the-moment recognition of operation type — itself a skill AI cannot supply (grounded; G4 skill implication).
- **Traces to.** A1, A4; G4 (sharpening G1).
- **(inferred)** — G4 is grounded; its placement as a discrete gating step is the overlay. The classification is admittedly imperfect and unevidenced (grounded; G4 known-costs).

### Phase 2 — Diverge cheaply, then select against your own ranking first

- **Human role.** Ask for many candidates — ten analysis plans, twenty proof angles, fifteen hypotheses — and read them as a *terrain map* of the trained distribution, noting where it is dense (well-trodden) and where it thins (the frontier, where the non-obvious move lives) (grounded; G13, A3). Then rank the candidates yourself, with a one-line written rationale each, *before* opening AI's ranking; only then ask AI to rank, and reconcile the divergences deliberately rather than deferring (grounded; G8). Separately, make at least one *offensive* use of A2: ask what technique from an adjacent field would apply here, then judge the transplant yourself (grounded; A2).
- **AI role.** Generate breadth at volume (grounded; A3, A4); propose cross-domain transplants (grounded; A2); rank and critique — but second, never first (grounded; A7, G8).
- **The empower-replace line.** The substantive contribution is **selection, not generation**. Generation is the cheap half; selection is the scarce human half AI structurally cannot supply, because retrospective selective retention requires accumulated taste AI lacks (grounded; A4, innovation note §2–3). Candidate *generation* is not the routine-syntax category G1 carves out as safe — it sits in a contested middle, fine to delegate *only because* the human then selects against their own judgment; if a reader takes the delegation license without the selection discipline, this becomes replacement (inferred from G1, A4). **Signal:** you are about to keep the candidate AI ranked first. **Test:** *could you defend this ranking without AI in six months?* (grounded; G1 heuristic, verbatim from guidelines.md line 55). If your only reason is that it read as fluent and confident, you have been anchored (grounded; A5, A7) — that is replace. If AI ranks first and you rubber-stamp, your taste never gets the direction-and-outcome reps it needs and you inherit AI's distribution bias as your own (inferred from G8, A4).
- **Skill built or preserved.** Selection / taste — discriminating a promising candidate from a merely fluent-novel one (grounded; G8 skill implication). The logged divergence between your ranking and AI's becomes, over time, a map of where your taste is reliable — a second-output instrument, not only a better-decision one (inferred from G8, G11).
- **Traces to.** A1, A2, A3, A4, A5, A7; G8, G11, G13.
- **(inferred)** — moves grounded; the rank-first procedure is the cleanest anti-anchoring mechanism in the set and is kept near-verbatim from the bottleneck lens.

### Phase 3 — Design, with verification promoted to the front

- **Human role.** Write the investigation's structure yourself — controls, the thing that would count as evidence. Decide what would convincingly verify *before* designing. Run an explicit pre-mortem prompt: *what would falsify this? what control is missing? what would a hostile reviewer say? three ways the result could be wrong?* (grounded; G9).
- **AI role.** Attack the design on command — the structured attack-prompt overrides its validation default (grounded; A6, A7, G9).
- **The empower-replace line.** The design's robustness and the choice of what counts as convincing verification stay human, because the system that would otherwise supply them is biased toward accepting its own output (grounded; A6). **Signal:** you are about to design first and bolt verification on later. **Test:** did the pre-mortem surface an attack you take seriously and act on? If you ran it and changed nothing, you used AI to validate, not to attack (grounded; G9).
- **Skill built or preserved.** Adversarial thinking / falsification habit (grounded; G9 skill implication).
- **Traces to.** A6, A7, A9; G9 (pairs with G3).
- **(inferred)** — moves grounded; verification-first placement is the cluster-II implication operationalized.

### Phase 4 — Execution, on tasks that resist decomposition

- **Scope note.** This phase covers two kinds of execution: producing the analysis/argument/code, and *generating empirical data* (instrument runs, fieldwork, wet-lab, data collection). The foundation's coverage is lighter on the second (grounded; step-0-landscape.md line 226), and most of what follows is written for the writing/analysis case. Empirical data generation is named here but not worked; it is a known gap, not a solved phase.
- **Human role.** Where a task decomposes cleanly into routine sub-parts, delegate freely and reclaim attention for what compounds. Where routine and original interleave in the same move, stay involved at fine grain — write the load-bearing pieces yourself, prompt AI for filling and polish, integrate and iterate (grounded; G4, G5).
- **AI role.** Parallel production engine for recombination; subordinate filler for non-decomposable work (grounded; A1, A3).
- **The empower-replace line.** This is the phase the line is hardest to locate, and the honesty about that must be carried here, not firewalled into caveats: A17 says the substantive and routine moves interleave *within a single sentence*, so "design is human, execution is AI" can fail mid-task (grounded; A17). The clean per-phase line is a fiction at this seam. The line *should* fall where "a parameter choice becomes a substantive claim about the world," but A17 predicts that point is not cleanly locatable in advance (grounded; A17). The operational consequence, stated plainly: the in-the-moment checkpoint can miss the substantive move, and the retrospective capability log (Phase 6) is what catches the drift afterward — which means the checkpoint architecture is weakest exactly where research value most concentrates. **Signal:** you are reaching for "should I just have AI do all of this?" and the answer is arriving by default rather than by decision (grounded; G5). The rule: refuse whole-task outsourcing on non-decomposable tasks even when slower; the "loss" only looks like loss if the second output is unmeasured (grounded; G5, A17).
- **Skill built or preserved.** Fine-grained involvement where routine and original interleave (grounded; G5 skill implication).
- **Traces to.** A1, A3, A17; G4, G5, G13.
- **(inferred)** — this is the phase whose coverage the process is weakest at, on both the conceptual seam and the empirical-data gap, and it says so in place.

### Phase 5 — Verify from an independent source

- **Human role.** For any output you will rely on substantively, apply at least one verification mode whose *source is independent of the generator*: formalize it, replicate from independent ground, or read critically with the source open (grounded; G3). Treat any AI literature output as a *starting graph*: source-walk each cited claim and mark it green / yellow / red; yellow and red do not become foundation without re-derivation (grounded; G10). Apply the same suspicion to the Phase-0b terrain map and to any AI landscape summary: as the floor rises, those summaries get more fluent and more trusted, which is exactly where A8 frontier-hallucination contaminates (grounded; A8 leaning-structural; the fluency→trust step is inferred from A5, A13, not asserted by A8 or G10).
- **AI role.** Generate candidates fast; assist replication mechanics. AI does *not* verify its own output (grounded; A6, A9).
- **The empower-replace line.** Verification is structurally human and non-negotiable, and it is the one stage whose line drifts *least* as AI improves — because A6 (self-validation) is "a category problem, not a capability problem": a generator cannot certify itself no matter how good it gets (grounded; A6, structural), and A9 (the verifiability gap) is structural and *widens* with capability — cheaper generation means more candidate output per unit of verification, so "the gap is bigger in the AI regime, not smaller" (grounded; A9, structural). One qualifier: part of the literature-verification load rides on A8, which is *ambiguous, leaning structural* and partly closing where retrieval helps (grounded; A8 tag), so the *literature-graph* portion of this phase's burden may shrink even though the A6/A9 core does not. **Signal:** you are about to ship an output whose only check is the model that produced it, or cite a paraphrase you have not walked to source. **Test:** can you name the independent verification mode applied?
- **Skill built or preserved.** Formalization, replication, critical reading of the literature graph — the part of the workflow where the researcher develops most (grounded; G3, G10; cluster-II direction in `02-implications.md`). Deepened periodically by a full replication of a recent paper, one per quarter (grounded; G14).
- **Traces to.** A5, A6, A8, A9, A13; G3, G10, G14.
- **(grounded)** at the level of the individual moves; the "drifts least" invariance claim is **(inferred)** but the foundation's structural tags for A6 and A9 support it strongly, with the A8 qualifier above carried explicitly.

### Phase 6 — Carry state, record provenance, instrument both outputs

- **Human role.** Maintain version-controlled project state designed to re-ground a fresh session (grounded; G2). Record model, prompt, parameters, and date alongside any relied-on output (grounded; G15). Periodically, ~15 minutes each, update two thin ledgers: a **capability log** ("what did I delegate that I might be losing the ability to do? what was hard a month ago and is easy now?") and a **calibration log** (expected vs. actual time, AI-assisted or not, felt-vs-measured help) (grounded; G6, G7).
- **AI role.** Consume state at session start; templates may automate the reproducibility metadata (grounded; G15 notes). AI must *not* author the self-assessment — that would defeat its purpose.
- **The empower-replace line.** This phase exists to keep the rest of the process *honest*: the second output is invisible by default, so the checkpoint cannot be audited unless development is tracked as deliberately as the deliverable (grounded; G6). **Signal:** a month where every task answer is "AI did it, I reviewed" — the drift flag the capability log exists to catch (grounded; G5 success). The calibration log answers cluster IV's 39-point felt-vs-measured gap so the checkpoint runs on data, not feeling (grounded; A13). The split inside this phase: the *re-grounding mechanics* (re-pasting context against literal session-forgetting) address A10/A11, which are *ambiguous* and on a closing track (grounded; A10 tag) — but *owned, curated, version-controlled state* is durable for reasons independent of AI memory (grounded; A10 note). The reproducibility logging (G15) addresses A12, the one *temporary* attribute: its relevance is expected to *decline* as logging standardizes and tooling absorbs it (grounded; A12 tag). Design so the declining-relevance parts can be handed to future memory and provenance tooling without dismantling the durable state-carrying part.
- **Skill built or preserved.** Carrier-of-cumulative-state craft; record-keeping discipline; the meta-skill of noticing one's own developing or atrophying capacity (grounded; G2, G6, G7, G15).
- **Traces to.** A10, A11, A12, A13, A16; G2, G6, G7, G15. (A15 — vibes-driven adoption — is a field-level cultural attribute about how productivity *claims propagate*, "cultural, not technical" (grounded; A15 tag, line 153), not about an individual's self-perception; it is the motivation for measuring at all, not a trace for this phase's mechanism.)
- **(grounded)** at the move level; **(inferred)** that these belong consolidated in one phase.

### Phase 7 — Prune the portfolio and protect exploration

- **Human role.** Because generation is cheap, the working unit tends toward a *portfolio* of threads rather than a single project. Periodically prune — kill threads on signals you have learned to read — and protect a fixed exploration share (e.g. one half-day a week) for work aimed at no current deliverable; protected, not residual (grounded; G11, G12).
- **AI role.** Track thread state across the portfolio; resurface dormant threads on request (grounded; G2).
- **The empower-replace line.** The kill/keep decision is selective retention — the BVSR half AI structurally cannot supply (grounded; innovation note §2). Outsourcing it hands the most consequential research judgment to a parallel exploitation engine. The exploitation gradient is "self-destructive in the long run" (grounded; March 1991, via innovation note §1), and AI sharpens it by making exploitation cheap, so the exploration share must be defended by decision (inferred from A4, G12).
- **Skill built or preserved.** Taste over time via a direction-and-outcome log; patience for uncertain, distant-payoff work (grounded; G11, G12).
- **Traces to.** A1, A4; G11, G12, G13.
- **(inferred)** — the pruning and exploration-protection moves are grounded; the *project → portfolio* shift is **(speculative)** as a claim about how research will actually reorganize.

### Phase 8 — Disseminate

- **Human role.** Decide the claim's reach and the caveats it carries; stand behind it. The rhetorical framing of a claim — how a talk or abstract positions it — is part of the judged contribution and is not cleanly separable from the claim itself (grounded; A17, applied here as in Phase 4).
- **AI role.** Adapt format across audiences (talk, abstract, thread) and translate vocabulary across fields (grounded; A2), under the same A17 caveat: where reformatting starts to carry the framing of the claim, the line tightens back toward the human.
- **The empower-replace line.** What you assert and how strongly stays human. "Reformatting is fair to offload" is true only to the extent the reformatting is *not* itself reframing — and A17 says that clean cut does not reliably exist (grounded; A17), so this phase inherits Phase 4's caveat rather than asserting a clean line. The substantive contribution is the claim and its framing, not its mechanical packaging.
- **Skill built or preserved.** Calibrated public assertion — knowing what you can defend, and in whose vocabulary.
- **Traces to.** A2, A17; G1.
- **(inferred)** — this is among the thinnest-grounded phases; current attributes address it least, and it should not be read as as-governed as verification.

### Phase 9 — Respond to review and revise

- **Human role.** Read the reviews, decide which critiques to accept, and write the substantive parts of the response and the revision yourself — the concessions, the rebuttals, the new analysis a reviewer demands. The judgment of *which* critique is right and *how far* to move is the contribution.
- **AI role.** Draft routine response scaffolding, surface where a reviewer's objection maps to a known weakness, format the rebuttal letter — subordinate to the human's decision on substance.
- **The empower-replace line.** An AI-drafted rebuttal that argues a position the researcher has not themselves reasoned to is replacement at the whole-job frame: defending the work under challenge is squarely a skill the job is judged on (grounded; G1, GOAL.md §The commitment). **Signal:** you are about to send a rebuttal whose argument you could not reconstruct under questioning.
- **Skill built or preserved.** Defending the work under adversarial challenge; the judgment of which criticism to concede.
- **Traces to.** A6, A7, A17; G1, G3, G9.
- **(inferred / sketched)** — the review/revision loop is a real lifecycle phase the foundation does not yet address directly; this phase is a reasoned placeholder, flagged as under-worked.

> **A note on the cadence.** It is tempting to map these phases onto a Monday-to-Friday week. The repo has no evidence for any cadence, and such a schedule contradicts both A17 (phases interleave within a single move) and the portfolio framing of Phase 7 (threads run in parallel, not in sequence). Any week-shaped illustration would be **(speculative)** and has not survived contact with an observed working session — the pilot domain is not yet chosen. The phases are a decomposition of *what the work contains*, not a timetable.

## Cross-cutting practices

Several disciplines run through every phase rather than living in one:

- **The whole-job empower-replace question (G1)** is the spine of all of it — asked at transitions, not keystrokes.
- **Classify before delegating (G4)** calibrates how strict the line is in every later phase.
- **Verify from an independent source (G3, G10), with replication as a periodic primitive (G14)** governs Phase 5 but also disciplines what enters Phase 0b's state file and Phase 2's candidate set.
- **Rank-before-AI (G8)** and **the direction-and-outcome log (G11)** are the selection discipline; they recur wherever candidates are chosen — Phases 0a, 2, and 7 — not only in Phase 2.
- **Resist the exploitation gradient (G12)** is a standing posture, surfaced in Phase 7 but pulling against every phase's pull toward fast generation.
- **External version-controlled state (G2)** and **standardized AI-provenance logging (G15)** are continuous record-keeping.
- **The two retrospective ledgers (G6 capability, G7 calibration)** are the process's self-audit; without them the empower/replace answer is a feeling with a documented 39-point error size (grounded; A13).
- **Refuse whole-task outsourcing on non-decomposable tasks (G5)** is the in-the-moment discipline of Phase 4 and the standing counterweight to clean per-phase lines.
- **AI as terrain map, not agent (G13)** is the posture in every phase where AI surveys (Phases 0a, 0b, 2, 5, 7, 8).

**Collaboration and mentorship — a cross-cutting consideration the phases above understate.** Every phase as drawn assumes a solo researcher, which is a simplification the foundation does not endorse. Three known cuts:

- **Human review as an independent-source verification mode.** G3 names "a colleague who has not seen the AI session" as one independent verification source (grounded; G3) — a co-author or reviewer is a stronger independent check than the researcher re-reading their own AI output, precisely because they do not share the session's anchoring (relates to A7).
- **Community power dynamics.** Pachter's concern that AI tooling can reshape who holds authority in a research community (grounded; step-0-landscape.md, Pachter entry) is a phase-independent risk the process does not yet address.
- **The mentorship / junior-researcher problem.** Mineault's formulation — "metacognition cannot develop when AI hides its mistakes" (grounded; step-0-landscape.md line 233) — is the sharpest version of the apprenticeship risk for novices, and it bears directly on the empower-replace question for anyone supervising trainees. It appears below as a "Bet" but is properly a standing consideration, not a one-line caveat.

**Candidate new guideline the process seems to need but no existing guideline covers.** Phase 6 and Phase 1 both rely on a discipline that is not currently a guideline: *scheduled re-examination of the foundation's own attribute tags against the current model generation* — explicitly deciding, on a cadence, which practices address now-closing limitations (re-grounding mechanics for A10/A11, raw-sycophancy guarding for the closing half of A7, reproducibility logging for A12) and can be retired as scaffolding, versus which trace to structural attributes that capability gains *widen* (selection A4, verification A9, perception gap A13, substitution asymmetry A16) and should absorb the design budget. A14 ("designed revisability") names the *need* for this but no G-numbered guideline operationalizes it as a recurring practice with a trigger and a cadence (confirmed against guidelines.md and Q1). 

One theoretical hook this candidate is tempting to claim — that *the durability axis and the substitution-risk axis are the same axis* — does **not** hold as stated **(speculative, and partly falsified)**. It is true for A4, A9, A16 (structural *and* high substitution risk), which is what makes the hook attractive. But A2 and A3 are tagged structural (maximally durable) and carry *no* substitution risk — they are capabilities to exploit, not skills at risk of being replaced (grounded; A2, A3 tags). The two axes are *correlated* in the cluster-V attributes the design budget most targets, not *identical*. A guideline along these lines would be a candidate for `add-guideline` once an attribute and a piece of evidence support it — flagged here, with the counterexample noted, not added.

## How it serves both outputs

Every phase is built to produce knowledge *and* capacity *and* a name-worthy artifact at once. The mechanism is *double-booking*: for each phase, ask in parallel — what knowledge does this yield, what capacity does it build, and is the artifact at a quality a researcher should put their name on? (inferred from GOAL.md §What changed about research two-outputs framing; *double-booking* is this draft's own coinage, not a sourced term).

| Phase | Knowledge / artifact (output 1) | Researcher (output 2) |
|---|---|---|
| 0a | A question worth pursuing, and an opportunity a less-equipped researcher would miss | Problem-selection and opportunity-spotting taste |
| 0b | A sharply framed question and a re-loadable state file | Framing-taste; logging judgment |
| 2 | A well-chosen direction, selected from breadth | Selection / taste; a logged map of where your taste is reliable |
| 3 | A falsifiable design with verification built in | The falsification habit |
| 4 | Load-bearing analysis and data the researcher can stand behind | Fine-grained involvement where routine and original interleave |
| 5 | A verified result — the artifact-quality gate that separates name-worthy work from slop (grounded; GOAL.md §What changed) | Verification craft — where the researcher develops most |
| 6 | Provenance and state that make the artifact reproducible and the claims auditable | The meta-skill of noticing one's own drift |
| 8 | A defensible, well-framed claim | Calibrated public assertion |
| 9 | A revision that answers the strongest objection | Defending the work under challenge |

The artifact column is not decoration: GOAL.md's claim that skill is "what separates AI slop from work a researcher should be willing to put their name on" means the verification gate (Phase 5) and the framing decisions (Phases 0b, 8) are where the artifact's quality is actually set, and where unskilled AI use produces fluent-but-below-standard work (grounded; GOAL.md §What changed about research). Phase 6 is the one phase whose deliverable *is* the second output, made a standing measurement rather than an emergent hope — because the failure mode is specifically silent and the researcher cannot trust their sense of either output (grounded; A13, GOAL.md §What changed about research "will not notice").

The reframe that makes the second output concrete: **the apprenticeship has a budget, and every delegation *of apprenticeship-bearing work* spends it** (inferred from GOAL.md §What changed about research, plus G1, G4, G5). The qualifier matters and corrects an easy overreach: GOAL.md is explicit that delegating skills *below* the judged frame is fine and does *not* draw the budget down (grounded; GOAL.md §The commitment). The "budget" is a useful framing, but its *finiteness* is itself a bet **(speculative)** — GOAL.md says the periphery *builds* the second output, not that there is a fixed quantity being spent; treat "budget" as a lens, not a measured quantity. Because the second output is built by exactly the peripheral work AI is best at, a pure-delegation framing sees only a time budget while the apprenticeship budget is drawn down in parallel, invisibly. This converts G5's "refuse whole-task outsourcing" from a caution into a positive allocation rule: *delegate routine that builds skills below the judged frame; protect routine that is the apprenticeship for skills at it.* And it gives the delegation decision a test the speed framing lacks — not "can AI do this faster?" but **"if AI does this, which apprenticeship am I declining, and is that a trade I can name and accept?"** (grounded; G1, frame-faithful sharpening).

This is the repo's positive construction of Vector 3: researcher growth is the criterion the process is accountable to, which the survey reports as a gap open across 47 entities (grounded; step-0-landscape.md line 209 — provisional per step-0-landscape.md §5).

## Honest limitations and what would test this

This process rests on bets. Each is paired with the evidence stream that would confirm or refute it.

- **Bet: the structural/temporary attribute tags hold.** The entire durability logic — invest the design budget where capability gains *widen* the human's role — rests on `01-ai-attributes.md`'s tags, which the file flags as a working hypothesis (Q1 tracks which hold). If a structural attribute (say A6 self-validation) is closed by an architecture that verifies independently of generation, Phase 5's invariance claim weakens (grounded caveat; A14). **Tested by:** the longitudinal self-study and the lab survey, observed across model generations.

- **Bet: the line is locatable in the moment.** A17 says the routine and original moves interleave inside a single sentence, so Phase 4's checkpoint can fire mid-task and be skipped under load; the process converts an in-the-moment judgment into a retrospective audit, which catches drift but does not prevent the slip (grounded; A17, G6). **Tested by:** observation of real working sessions, where the seam either is or isn't navigable.

- **Bet: the second output is measurable at all.** Phases 5 and 6 lean on capability and calibration logs, but G6's own notes concede "the right practice for this is genuinely unclear" (grounded; G6 notes, Q11). The ledgers risk becoming ritual that produces the *feeling* of tracking growth without the substance — the same felt-vs-actual trap they exist to counter (inferred from A13). **Tested by:** the n=1 self-experiment accumulating over a year (grounded; A13 implication, G7).

- **Bet: the artifact and the researcher are usually complementary.** GOAL.md asserts the benefit is "double" — better output now *and* growing capacity (grounded; GOAL.md §What changed about research) — but a deadline can force shipping the artifact by spending apprenticeship the researcher would rather protect; the process names the tension but offers no allocation rule for when the artifact must win **(speculative)**. **Tested by:** the pilot research project, where the conflict either bites or doesn't.

- **Bet: the apparatus earns its overhead.** It is heaviest exactly where it is least needed — for cleanly decomposable work, double-booking every phase buys little, and Q12 already flags that G5 "may not generalize across all research domains" (grounded; G5 notes, Q12). And "which apprenticeship am I declining?" can be answered "this one" about anything, licensing make-work; only active application of the moving-line guard (skills below the judged frame are fine to replace) keeps it honest (grounded; GOAL.md §The commitment). **Tested by:** calibration data (G7) distinguishing protected apprenticeship from indulged inefficiency.

- **Bet: it survives the rising floor.** As the unskilled-AI floor rises and the boundary climbs, the *first* output gets cheaper to reach without skill, which makes the *second* the more durable reason for deliberateness; whatever the boundary's current height, "which capacity did this build?" still discriminates empower from replace (grounded; G1, GOAL.md §What changed about research trajectory note). The novice case is a real cost the process does not hide: for a researcher whose taste is currently worse than the model's output, Phases 0a–3 trade short-term output quality for long-term capacity — the wager empower-not-replace stakes itself on (grounded; GOAL.md §What changed about research; Mineault's metacognition problem, step-0-landscape.md line 233). **Out of scope by construction:** the regime where AI runs the loop with no human in it; every checkpoint presumes a human to reach it (grounded; GOAL.md §What changed about research regime exclusion).

**Open questions this raises.** (1) Does any guideline operationalize A14's "designed revisability" as a recurring practice, or is the scheduled-retirement discipline a genuine gap (candidate new guideline, above)? (2) Is the *project → portfolio* shift (Phase 7) real, or does research remain project-shaped with parallelism inside it? (3) Where exactly does the Phase 4 seam become navigable in practice — is there an in-the-moment scaffold beyond willpower-plus-retrospective-audit? (4) How should the process handle empirical data generation (Phase 4) and external peer review (Phase 9), the two lifecycle phases it currently only sketches? (5) Should the repo adopt, refine, or contest the centaur/cyborg vocabulary before more guidelines touch it (a decision the maintainer has deferred; grounded; step-0-landscape.md line 234)? None of these is resolved here; they are surfaced for the inquiry, not decided.
